‚úàÔ∏è Final Year Project (TP): AR Wayfinding at Changi Airport

During my diploma studies in Aviation Management at Temasek Polytechnic, I worked on a Final Year Project in collaboration with Changi Airport Group (CAG). The project explored the feasibility of implementing Augmented Reality (AR) wayfinding solutions to enhance passenger navigation within airport terminals.

Large international airports such as Changi Airport serve millions of passengers annually, and first-time or transit passengers often experience difficulty navigating terminals efficiently. Traditional static signage may not always provide intuitive or personalised guidance, especially in complex layouts. The project aimed to evaluate whether AR-based navigation could improve wayfinding efficiency and overall passenger experience.

Technical Approach & Software Used

Although the project originated from a non-computing discipline, it involved several technical and analytical components.

Key technologies and software used included:

  • Python
    • Used for basic scripting and logic development
    • Simulated routing logic, distance calculations, and directional guidance
    • Assisted in processing structured data related to navigation paths
  • AR Development & Prototyping Tools
    • Mobile AR frameworks for concept prototyping
    • 3D spatial mapping concepts to represent indoor environments
    • User interaction design for visual overlays and directional cues
  • Data & Productivity Tools
    • Microsoft Excel for data collection, comparison of navigation paths, and analysis
    • Presentation and prototyping tools to communicate design concepts to stakeholders

The emphasis of the project was not on building a full production system, but on assessing concept feasibility, user experience, and operational value in a real-world airport setting.

Outcomes & Learning

This project provided early exposure to: - translating real-world operational challenges into technical concepts, - applying data and logic to support navigation systems, - collaborating with an industry partner (CAG), - and communicating technical ideas clearly to non-technical stakeholders.

The experience sparked my interest in technology-driven solutions and laid the foundation for my subsequent pursuit of applied computing.
